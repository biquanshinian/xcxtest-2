Subject: Proposal for Public Engagement — Lunar Wishes from China for Artemis IV

Dear NASA Office of Communications / Public Engagement Division,

My name is Yuze Hu, and I am the founder of Mars Explorer Log,
a space exploration community based in China with 2,000 passionate
members who share a deep love for humanity's journey to the stars.

First, I would like to express our most sincere gratitude to NASA
and the outstanding Artemis program team. The successful completion
of the Artemis II mission has inspired millions of space enthusiasts
in China and around the world.

I am writing to propose a collaborative public engagement initiative
for the upcoming Artemis IV mission. The project is called
"Lunar Wishes" — an effort to collect digital blessings and messages
from Chinese space enthusiasts and deliver them electronically to the
Moon as part of the Artemis IV mission.

PROJECT OVERVIEW:
- We have built a digital platform (WeChat Mini Program) to collect
  text-based wishes and names from participants across China.
- All data will be compressed into a minimal-size digital package
  (text-only, estimated under 1MB even for tens of thousands of
  entries).
- We respectfully seek NASA's authorization to include this data
  package in any publicly accessible digital payload or commemorative
  data carried on the Artemis IV mission.
- Each participant receives a personalized digital "boarding pass"
  as a keepsake, enhancing public engagement and emotional connection.

WHY THIS MATTERS:
- Space exploration belongs to all of humanity. This project builds
  a cultural bridge, bringing the enthusiasm of the Chinese public
  for lunar exploration into the global Artemis narrative.
- It demonstrates that the wonder of space transcends borders and
  unites people around the world through shared dreams.
- Our platform already provides real-time tracking of NASA missions,
  including Artemis II telemetry data, NASA's Astronomy Picture of
  the Day (APOD), and Earth Observatory Natural Event Tracker (EONET)
  data, serving as a bridge between NASA's work and Chinese space
  enthusiasts.

TECHNICAL DETAILS:
- Data format: UTF-8 encoded text (name + message + location)
- Estimated final package size: < 1MB (compressed)
- No executable code, no binary data — pure text only
- We can deliver data in any format NASA specifies (JSON, CSV, etc.)
- All content undergoes both automated and manual moderation

ABOUT OUR PLATFORM:
- Platform: WeChat Mini Program (火星探索日志 / Mars Explorer Log)
- Active users: 2,000 space enthusiasts
- Key features: SpaceX launch tracking, Starlink satellite tracking,
  Artemis II real-time telemetry, NASA data integration
- Contact: huyuzetongxue@163.com

We are fully prepared to comply with any NASA guidelines, data format
requirements, and review processes. We welcome the opportunity to
discuss this proposal further at your convenience.

IMPORTANT NOTE:
This is a purely civilian, non-governmental, non-technical public
engagement initiative. It involves no technology transfer, no
government-to-government cooperation, and no access to any controlled
information. Its sole purpose is to carry the dreams and good wishes
of ordinary people to the Moon.

Thank you for considering this initiative. We firmly believe that the
next time humans set foot on the Moon, it should carry the dreams of
as many people as possible — and what could be more romantic than that?

Warm regards,

Yuze Hu
Social Practitioner
WeChat Mini Program: Mars Explorer Log (火星探索日志)
huyuzetongxue@163.com
